The Interceptor: Automated River Cleaning

The Interceptor is a technological breakthrough in the fight against marine plastic. Engineering studies show that 80% of ocean plastic enters from just 1% of rivers. By targeting these "arteries" of pollution, we can stop the crisis at the source. The Interceptor uses a catamaran hull and a solar-powered conveyor belt to lift trash into dumpsters. This allows the system to operate 24/7 without a crew, making it the most cost-effective STEM solution in maritime history.

Ganges Case Study: Biological Failure

In the Ganges, the pollution has exceeded the river's natural ability to self-clean. With over 4.8 billion liters of sewage flowing in daily, the biological oxygen demand (BOD) has spiked, leading to massive dead zones. This research highlights the critical need for Chemical Engineering and Sewage Treatment Plants (STPs) rather than just symbolic legal status for the water.

Blue Carbon: UK Seagrass Meadows

Seagrass is one of the most efficient carbon sinks on the planet. In the UK, restoring seagrass could offset millions of tonnes of CO2. This biological STEM solution focuses on reforestation of the sea floor, providing habitats for seahorses and cod while protecting our coastline from erosion.
