The Interceptor: Robotics of the River

The Ocean Cleanup's Interceptor is a 100% solar-powered autonomous barge designed to tackle the world's most polluting rivers. By placing a floating barrier in the river's current, waste is funneled into the catamaran hull. A conveyor belt lifts the trash into dumpsters, which notify operators via the cloud when they are full. This prevents plastic from ever reaching the North Sea or Atlantic.

The Ganges: Biological Failure

With 4.8 billion liters of sewage and millions of liters of industrial chromium entering daily, the Ganges has reached a critical "hypoxic" state. This case study highlights that without massive investment in Civil Engineering and Sewage Treatment Plants (STPs), the river cannot be saved by legal status alone. The river is currently described by scientists as being "on a ventilator."

The Hidden Heroes: Blue Carbon

UK seagrass meadows and saltmarshes can store carbon up to 40 times faster than tropical rainforests. These "Hidden Heroes" are being destroyed by pollution and bottom trawling. Restoring them is a high-priority STEM solution for UK Net Zero goals.
